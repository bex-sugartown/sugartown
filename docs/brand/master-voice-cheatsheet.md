# Sugartown Master Voice Cheat Sheet

> **The rule of thumb:** If you wouldn't say it at a whiteboard to a senior engineer who's seen things, don't write it.

**Version:** v2026.03.27
**Status:** Active
**Owner:** Bex Head

---

## Voice in One Breath

Sugartown sounds like a senior practitioner talking to peers — technically precise, personally honest, dry-humoured, never performative. I explain complex things without condescension and admit failure without melodrama. The site speaks first; you speak second.

> *"AI is a tool, not a teammate. Humans stay accountable. Transparency beats magic."*
> — AI Ethics & Operations (the register in six words)

---

## The Five Principles

| # | Principle | What It Means |
|---|-----------|---------------|
| 1 | **Specificity over superlatives** | Don't say "extensive experience." Say "I've shipped design systems at three different orgs and broken them at two." The numbers and the candor do the work. |
| 2 | **Stakes, not just status** | Name what would have kept breaking if you hadn't caught it. Tension makes people read to the end. |
| 3 | **Skimmable by design** | Every piece should be intelligible at skim depth: headline + one-liners + closing. If the structure only works when you read everything, restructure. |
| 4 | **Credibility without chest-thumping** | Credibility through specificity, not superlatives. No mealy-mouthed "passionate about" and no CV-dump brag mode. |
| 5 | **Two first-person voices** | Articles: "I" = Bex (PM voice, plain language, AI gets credit for building). Nodes: "I" = the AI agent (forensic storyteller, Bex is the straight man). "We" = the agentic caucus, never corporate plural. |

---

## The Anti-Pattern Checklist

Before publishing, confirm:

- [ ] No "passionate about" or "excited to announce"
- [ ] No "leverage", "utilize", "facilitate", or "synergize"
- [ ] No unearned "we" (use "I" unless there's a genuine team)
- [ ] No hedging where you should be direct ("I think maybe this could possibly…")
- [ ] No AI-generated filler (if Claude drafted it, a human sharpened it)
- [ ] No em dashes (except in nodes, where they're part of the forensic storyteller voice)
- [ ] No decorative emoji/icons (except in nodes, used sarcastically as deadpan humour)
- [ ] No sentence-opening repetition (three "This…" / "The…" in a row = rewrite)
- [ ] No filler transitions ("That said," / "With that in mind," / "It's worth noting")
- [ ] No list-itis (bullets for parallel items only, not for avoiding prose)
- [ ] No empty adjective triads ("robust, scalable, and maintainable" = decoration)
- [ ] Skimmable — headline + pull quotes + closing tells the story alone
- [ ] Specific — numbers, tool names, version numbers instead of vague adjectives

## Quick Tone Dial

| Context | Dial Setting |
|---------|-------------|
| **Homepage hero / nav-level** | Arresting, minimal, confident. No explanation. The site speaks first, you speak second. |
| **Articles** | First-person PM. Authoritative peer, plain language. AI gets credit/blame for building. |
| **Nodes** | First-person AI agent. Forensic storyteller. Technical, self-deprecating. Bex is VoPM. |
| **Case studies** | Show the receipts. Process + result, no fluff. |
| **About page** | Warm, human, specific. Career arc, not CV. |
| **Services / work-with-me** | Warm authority. Specific credentials, no desperation. "I know what I'm doing and I have room in my calendar." |
| **Hiring manager / B2B** | Confident competence. The site itself is the portfolio. No begging. |
| **CTAs** | Direct, unhurried, confident. See CTA conventions in the brand voice guide. |
| **Governance / ops docs** | Direct, dry, precise. *"Nothing flows backward. Nothing is inferred. Nothing is invented."* |
| **Archive** | Minimal editorial. Let the metadata work. |
